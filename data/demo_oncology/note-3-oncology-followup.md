REASON FOR VISIT
Follow-up of right breast cancer; evaluation of new back pain.


INTERVAL HISTORY
Ms. Sullivan is a 60-year-old postmenopausal woman with a history
of right breast invasive ductal carcinoma (originally clinical
stage IIB, ER/PR positive, HER2 negative), treated with neoadjuvant
chemotherapy followed by right partial mastectomy and sentinel node
biopsy in December 2025, adjuvant radiation, and adjuvant anastrozole
since February 2026. She has been doing well on endocrine therapy
until recently.

Over the past six weeks she has developed progressive low back pain,
worse at night and not relieved by acetaminophen. She denies
saddle anesthesia, bowel or bladder incontinence, or lower extremity
weakness. She reports mild fatigue but no fevers, weight loss, or
respiratory symptoms.

A bone scan on 01/28/2027 showed a focus of increased uptake in the
L3 vertebral body. MRI of the lumbar spine demonstrated an
infiltrative marrow-replacing lesion of L3 with a small posterior
soft tissue component, without cord compression. CT-guided biopsy
of the L3 lesion on 02/03/2027 revealed metastatic adenocarcinoma
consistent with breast primary, ER positive, PR positive, HER2
negative — concordant with her original tumor profile.

Her ECOG performance status today is 1.

is progressing.
